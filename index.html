  • CANNABIS OIL & DRUG TRAFFICKING: MUHAMMAD LUKMAN MOHAMAD V. PP [2021] 7 CLJ 524

    News Straits Times (NST) - ‘Muhammad’s lawyer, Farhan Maaruf, was quoted as saying that the former only wanted to help patients suffering from ailments, and that he was not distributing cannabis on the streets’.

    Le Cannabiste, UK - ‘Special thanks to Farhan Maaruf for saving a man’s life’

    ‘Also, I have a lot of gratitude and appreciation for my lawyer Hisyam Teh and team, and also Farhan Maaruf who stuck with me from the High Court’ – Muhammad Lukman.

    One of the most notable cases in his career was the successful defense of Muhammad Lukman. Lukman was arrested in Dec 2015 for possessing of 3.1 liters of cannabis oil, 279 grams of compressed cannabis, and 1.4kg of substance containing tetrahydrocannabinol (THC). On 30 August 2018, the High Criminal Court (6) of Shah Alam ruled that Muhammad Lukman was sentenced to death by hanging and attracted international attention with coverage in France, the United Kingdom, and Australia.

    On the defense side, Farhan argued that Lukman was not involved in any crime and was not a criminal who threatened national security. Throughout the trial, Farhan brought witnesses who had received medicinal cannabis oil from him. He maintains that Lukman sold the substance not for a mind-altering effect, but to be used medicinally. On 17 February 2021, the Federal Court overturned his conviction on two counts of drug trafficking, amending the charges to possession under Section 9 of the Dangerous Drugs Act (DDA).

  • DRUG TRAFFICKING ACQUITTED AND DISCHARGED

    News Straits Times (NST) - ‘Deputy public prosecutor Muhammad Hidayat Wahab prosecuted the case while Yuslaili was represented by counsel Sukhaimi Mashud, Iman by counsel G.Subramaniam Nair and Selvanteran Segaran while Farhan Maaruf for Izatul.’

    In 2019, the High Court exonerated three individuals from the capital charges of drug trafficking and possession. The trio, initially indicted under Section 39(B)(1)(a) of the Dangerous Drugs Act, faced the possibility of the death penalty for allegedly distributing 286.37 grams of MDMA, a controlled substance.

    However, Yuslaili Ahmad, Izatul Afiqah Khairuddin, and Iman Suhatman were ultimately absolved of all charges. The court's decision came after evaluating the testimonies of 10 witnesses and the persuasive arguments presented by their adept legal counsel, leading to the conclusion that the prosecution had not established a compelling case against them.

  • CHILD ABUSE & NEGLIGENCE: SITI BAINUN AHD RAZALI LWN PENDAKWA RAYA [2023] MLJU 1710

    News Straits Times (NST) - ‘Judge Izralizam Sanusi granted the temporary release from today until Feb 11 after hearing submissions from Farhan Maaruf who is representing Siti Bainun and Deputy Public Prosecutor, Zilfinaz Abbas.’

    Siti Bainun Ahd Razali, the founder of the Rumah Bonda welfare home in Malaysia faced charges of neglect and abuse, specifically for child neglect under Section 31(1)(a) of the Child Act 2001 and causing physical and emotional harm to Bella, a teenage girl with down syndrome who was under her care. Siti Bainun was accused of severely neglecting and abusing Bella, resulting in public outcry and legal action.

    Legal proceedings featured testimonies from medical professionals, caretakers, and other residents of Rumah Bonda, scrutinizing evidence of Bella's injuries and living conditions. Siti Bainun was ultimately charged with neglect and abuse, and the court's ruling on this case is seen as a critical step in reinforcing child welfare protections in Malaysia.
